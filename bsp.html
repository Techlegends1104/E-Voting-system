Bahujan Samaj Party (BSP)

Mayawati

Mayawati (born 15 January 1956) is an Indian politician who is the founder and leader of the Bahujan Samaj Party (BSP). She has served as the Chief Minister of Uttar Pradesh four times. Known for her strong advocacy for Dalit rights, Mayawati’s leadership has been pivotal in shaping the political landscape of Uttar Pradesh. She has focused on social justice, uplifting backward classes, Dalits, and minorities through her policies and leadership.

Satish Chandra Mishra

Satish Chandra Mishra (born 24 January 1952) is an Indian politician and a key leader of the Bahujan Samaj Party. He serves as the national general secretary of the party and has played a significant role in BSP’s strategic decisions and political campaigns. Mishra is a prominent leader who works alongside Mayawati in formulating the party's policies and strengthening its base across India.

Ashok Alok

Ashok Alok is a senior leader of the Bahujan Samaj Party who has been instrumental in strengthening the party’s organizational structure. He has represented the party at various levels and has worked tirelessly for the rights of Dalits and backward classes. Alok is known for his leadership in mobilizing support for the BSP in various parts of India.

S.C. Mishra

S.C. Mishra is another prominent leader in the Bahujan Samaj Party who has contributed to the ideological development of the party. Mishra has played a key role in the BSP’s outreach programs, focusing on social justice and the empowerment of the marginalized communities across the country.

Rahul Bahuguna

Rahul Bahuguna is a rising leader in the Bahujan Samaj Party known for his involvement in strengthening the party’s presence in Uttar Pradesh and other regions. Bahuguna is committed to carrying forward the legacy of Mayawati and working towards the upliftment of backward classes, Dalits, and other marginalized groups in India.
