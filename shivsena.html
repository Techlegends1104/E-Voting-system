Shiv Sena

Bal Thackeray

Bal Keshav Thackeray (23 January 1926 – 17 November 2012) was an Indian politician and the founder of the Shiv Sena. He was a prominent figure in the politics of Maharashtra and was known for his strong advocacy for the rights of the Marathi-speaking people. Thackeray’s leadership and ideology focused on regional pride, Hindutva, and the empowerment of local Maharashtrians. Under his leadership, Shiv Sena became a major political force in Maharashtra.

Uddhav Thackeray

Uddhav Thackeray (born 27 July 1960) is the current Chief Minister of Maharashtra and the leader of the Shiv Sena. He is the son of Bal Thackeray and took over the party's leadership after his father's demise. Uddhav's leadership has seen Shiv Sena evolve from a regional party to a major player in national politics. He has been known for his role in forming the Maha Vikas Aghadi coalition, which included the Nationalist Congress Party (NCP) and the Indian National Congress (INC) in 2019.

Aditya Thackeray

Aditya Thackeray (born 13 June 1990) is the son of Uddhav Thackeray and the grandson of Bal Thackeray. He is a prominent youth leader of Shiv Sena and has served as the Minister of Tourism and Environment in Maharashtra. Known for his progressive views, Aditya has brought a modern, youthful approach to Shiv Sena's politics while staying rooted in its traditional ideals.

Arvind Sawant

Arvind Sawant is a senior leader of Shiv Sena and has served as the Member of Parliament from Mumbai South. He has also held the position of Union Minister for Heavy Industries and Public Enterprises in the Government of India. Sawant is known for his strong leadership and advocacy for the welfare of Mumbai's working-class people and urban development.

Anil Parab

Anil Parab is a senior leader of the Shiv Sena and currently serves as the Minister of Transport in Maharashtra. He is known for his contributions to infrastructure development in Maharashtra, particularly in the transportation sector. Parab is also a key figure in the administration of the party's affairs in Maharashtra.
